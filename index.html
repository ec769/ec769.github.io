Erica Cai


I am a fourth year PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. I am a member of UMass NLP and the Statistical Social Language Analysis lab and am advised by Prof. Brendan T. O'Connor. My research interests are broadly in the intersection of natural language processing, computational social science, and knowledge graphs. Currently, I am working on low resource event extraction and other types of information extraction (relations, named entities).

Previously in graduate school, I worked with Prof. David Jensen and my research was in causal structure learning, causal inference, and explainable artificial intelligence. My publications and projects are in Research. Before graduate school, I worked with Prof. Eric Allender in computational complexity theory and with Prof. Janne Lindqvist in developing methods to explore the privacy and security of ephemeral messages.
